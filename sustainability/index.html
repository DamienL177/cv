SUSTAINABILITY & CIVIC ENGAGEMENT

Sustainable Development Ideation Competition

On the 25th January 2024, my group and I participated in class to a little tournament organised by our Communication and Management teachers. Among our class, we made groups with 4 to 6 individuals and we thought of a project we could do around the 17 objectives given by the United Nations. We then had to prepare a poster and present our project to our class. To judge those presentations, our teachers invited Marc ELIAS, founder of Hall of Change. The groups would then be given one vote each and the teachers and guest two votes each.
As can be seen by the certificate, my group made a better presentation than the other 5 of the class which were all really interesting.



The project I worked on and might use for part of my civic engagement is called Compo7. You can find our poster by pushing the button under the certificate. According to a law passed in 2020, we are all supposed to separate our organic waste from the household rubbish since January 2024. The main use of organic waste is making compost. While considering this fact, my group realised that there was a lot yet for people to understand about compost. We intend to help students learn all they can about it through two work groups. One would be theoretical and the other a bit more practical.
I invite you to look at our posted, and you'll find more informations along with a way to contact the group if you want to know more.
